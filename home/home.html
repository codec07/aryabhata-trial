Aaryabhata

Aryabhata was an ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer who lived during the 5th century CE. He is considered one of the most influential scholars in the field of mathematics and astronomy in ancient India. Aryabhata's major work, "Aryabhatiya," is a seminal text that significantly advanced the understanding of mathematics and astronomy in the Indian subcontinent.

These tables were fundamental in the computation of astronomical and mathematical problems, and they greatly influenced later Indian mathematicians' work in this area.

Solar System: Aaryabhata's work in astronomy was groundbreaking, and he offered a revolutionary heliocentric model of the solar system, long before Nicolaus Copernicus proposed a similar idea in the 16th century. He also correctly explained the phenomenon of solar and lunar eclipses, showcasing his profound understanding of celestial mechanics.

He provided an accurate value for π as √10 (approximately 3.1622), and he also mentioned that this value was an approximation. While his calculation wasn't as precise as later mathematicians' efforts, it was a remarkable attempt considering the limited mathematical tools available at that time.
